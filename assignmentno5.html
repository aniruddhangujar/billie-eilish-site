Billie Eilish Pirate Baird O’Connell is a genre-defying singer, songwriter, and creative visionary who has reshaped the music industry with her raw honesty, haunting sound, and fearless individuality. Born on December 18, 2001, in Los Angeles, California, Billie was raised in a family of musicians and actors, which allowed her to begin writing and recording music at a very young age alongside her brother and collaborator, Finneas O’Connell.

She first gained attention in 2015 when she uploaded "Ocean Eyes" to SoundCloud—a song written and produced by Finneas. The track quickly went viral, and by the time she released her debut EP "don’t smile at me" in 2017, Billie had already begun building a massive global following. Her full-length debut album "WHEN WE ALL FALL ASLEEP, WHERE DO WE GO?" (2019) was a cultural phenomenon, earning her five Grammy Awards, including Album of the Year, Record of the Year, and Best New Artist—all before she turned 20.

Known for her unique voice, genre-bending production, and deeply personal lyrics, Billie creates music that speaks to themes of identity, mental health, heartbreak, and self-discovery. Her second album, "Happier Than Ever" (2021), further showcased her artistic evolution—blending vintage influences with modern storytelling and emotional depth.

Beyond music, Billie is also a fashion icon, known for her bold, oversized style that challenges conventional beauty standards and redefines what pop stars look like. She’s outspoken about body positivity, mental health awareness, and environmental issues, using her platform to inspire change and advocate for authenticity.

Billie Eilish isn’t just a singer—she’s a movement. With multiple Grammy wins, an Academy Award for Best Original Song ("No Time To Die" for the James Bond film), and millions of loyal fans around the world, she continues to push boundaries and evolve as an artist, always staying true to herself.
